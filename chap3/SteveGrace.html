G'Day from Steve

Pictured:The black and yellow mud dauber at work on the exterior wall of our home.

BUILDING DREAMS

Unless the LORD builds the house, the builders labor in vain.
Unless the LORD watches over the city, the guards stand watch in vain. Psalm 127:1.

I spent a few minutes fixated on the amazing activity of a black and yellow mud dauber a few days ago. This wasp was building her dream nest on a solid brick wall. It comprised of a series of cylindrical cells made from mud. After building a cell, the female wasp captures several spiders. The captured prey are stung and paralyzed before being placed in the nest, and then a single egg is deposited on the prey within each cell. The wasp then seals the cell with mud. After finishing a series of cells, she leaves and does not return. Eventually, the hatching larva will eat the prey and emerge from the nest.  It is miraculous to see God's creative power, even in the handiwork of a wasp. She not only builds a nest for her children but supplies the food for their survival.

We were born to dream, created to build our lives as best we can. Jesus came to bring us life!!.. And yet we live in a world where corrupt governments and civil conflicts erode hope and vision for the ordinary person. This past week in Australia we have seen our own government cower into deception and unethical ways simply to maintain a manipulative control over the people. Yet when we as a nation choose godless people to lead us then the walls of our cities are unsafe. It is impossible to build if your foundations are unstable.

Jesus knew their thoughts and said to them, "Every kingdom divided against itself will be ruined, and every city or household divided against itself will not stand. Matthew 12:25

I was inspired by a hard working wasp this week and deeply disappointed by the people leading my country. The Bible clearly tells us that the people will suffer greatly when evil people are placed in positions of power. Pray with me for our government.

Godspeed,

TV SERIES UPDATE

Pictured: The Every Town Down Under logo

We have developed the Outback Skies television series idea into "Everytown Downunder. " 

The website is www.everytowndownunder.com

The series will now focus on Steve more than ever, and will focus on Steve’s mission to visit every town in Australia with the Gospel.  Outback Skies still exists in a secular form to help us raise the funds we need to complete this project, and to evangelise using entertainment.

This past week, I was privileged to attend the NRB Convention in Nashville, Tennessee and present Everytown Downunder to a number of television networks and DVD distributors.  In addition to this, Geoff Bentley at the Australian Christian Channel has provided us with broadcast quality historical footage of Steve’s 1997 Tour around Australia.

We have had much interest in our work, with TV Channels based in the USA, UK, Germany, Russia, Romania and Holland all showing a keen interest in working with us to complete this exciting venture.

To help us out online, please visit the Outback Skies Facebook page and click on ‘Like’.  The more people we have following our work, the easier it will be to raise the funds needed to complete this project and continue to get the message our there.

HARLEY FOR SALE

NASHVILLE RECORDING OR BUST!!!

PIctured: 2007 Gretsch Electric (1959 replica)

The song "Big Dreams" recorded on the album "Children of the Western World" back in 1988 went on to become a #1 single on the American Christian radio charts in August 1990. It was a song of faith and big dreams for little people, focussing on the character of Abraham in the book of Genesis.

Well we will be re-recording "Big Dreams" in a classic Nashville country style in the coming weeks. But this time Jordan Grace and Trentan Kruger of the "SkyPilot" will be joining Steve for this classic song. This will be a great way to celebrate God's faithfulness over the past 25 years of serving the Lord in music ministry.

If you can help with the project gifts in the USA to World Missions are tax deductible. Thanks so much.
